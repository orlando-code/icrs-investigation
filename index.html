The ICRS 2026 delegate explorer

An interactive investigation of who came from where with what associated emissions, and how everyone fits together.

  • Map – speaker affiliations; search by name, institution, or talk keyword.
  • Emissions – estimated return-trip CO₂e to Auckland (economy flights where coming from overseas or further than 400 km from Auckland).
  • Network – co-authorship links between speakers; highlight people working in the same topics e.g. bleaching, OAE.

This is a simple tool and may contain errors. Contact me (Orlando) at rt582@cam.ac.uk if you find any issues.

Regarding the emissions – from my hotel room in Auckland it would be incredibly hypocritical of me to say that we shouldn't fly to conferences. They are invaluable opportunities to learn, collaborate, and share knowledge. But we must carefully consider the carbon cost of these connections and do everything we can to minimise our emissions in the rest of our lives. If this is something you think about a lot, I'd love to hear from you!

Methods

Data sources

Speaker names, affiliations, and talk metadata come from the ICRS 2026 programme and delegate list emailed to all participants. Affiliations are geocoded to institutional locations (country-level coordinates are used where a precise campus location is unavailable).

Travel emissions

Return-trip CO₂e is estimated per attendee using the emissions.dev API. This uses a combination of emission factors and routing data to estimate the average emissions for a given journey between two points. For everyone further than 400 km from Auckland, the journey is assumed to be by plane. Given the deficit of trains in New Zealand, the non-plane overground emissions are assumed to be by car with an occupancy of 1.54 (the mean occupancy in urban areas).

Network

Nodes are speakers or affiliations; edges connect people who share authorship on at least one ICRS talk. Node size reflects how many talks that speaker or affiliation appears on (author-list count, not co-authorship weight). Keyword search highlights nodes whose talks mention a term in the title or abstract, plus their co-authors on the graph.

Limitations

  • Geocoding is imperfect; some affiliations fall back to country centroids.
  • A couple of hundred delegates could not be geocoded from their affiliation titles – these were excluded from the analysis. The exclusion of so many attendees makes this a conservative estimate of total emissions.
  • Travel emissions are mean values – actual route travelled will significantly affect the value.
  • Emissions do not include local transport, accommodation, or conference operations – although we were told that the conference centre, NZICC is EarthCheck Silver certified...
  • The delegate/author lists are likely not completely up to date e.g. cancellations or changes to affiliations.
  • The co-authorship network shows shared programme authorship, not citation or collaboration history outside ICRS.
